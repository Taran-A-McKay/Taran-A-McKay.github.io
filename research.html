Research & Projects

My work sits at the intersection of cognitive science and artificial intelligence. I’m particularly interested in how intelligent systems learn, reason, and interact—both in controlled experimental settings and more complex multi-agent environments.

During my undergraduate studies, I explored topics ranging from theory of mind in large language models to reinforcement learning behavior and AI safety. I approach these problems from both a research and computational perspective, designing experiments and building systems to test them.

Codenames as an Interactive Test for LLM Theory of Mind

Undergraduate thesis exploring whether large language models exhibit theory of mind through an interactive game-based evaluation.

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning Experiments (VMAS)

Designed and ran experiments in a simulated multi-agent environment to study coordination and emergent behavior.

Research Assistant — AI Safety (Uncontrollable)

Contributed research toward a book on artificial superintelligence and global AI safety challenges.

To Bluff or Not to Bluff — Deep RL in Strategic Environments

Developed reinforcement learning agents capable of strategic bluffing behavior in competitive environments.

Snake World — Perverse Incentives in RL

Early research project investigating how reinforcement learning agents exploit poorly designed reward structures.
